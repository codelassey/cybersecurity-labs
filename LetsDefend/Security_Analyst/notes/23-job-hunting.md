# Job Hunting

## What to Expect as a SOC Analyst
- SOC analysts monitor, investigate, and respond to security alerts.
- Work is fast-paced, requires critical thinking, and often operates 24/7.
- Career progression typically goes from **Tier 1 (Monitoring)** to **Tier 2 (Incident Response)** and beyond.

---

# Tier 1 SOC Analyst (Security Analyst)

### Main Responsibilities
- Monitor security dashboards and alerts.
- Investigate triggered alerts.
- Determine alert severity.
- Identify True Positives vs False Positives.
- Escalate complex incidents to Tier 2.
- Block malicious IOCs when required.
- Improve SIEM detection rules by reducing false positives.
- Provide feedback on security gaps.

### Required Skills
- Continuous monitoring
- Networking fundamentals
- Security fundamentals
- Time management
- Communication
- Ability to work under pressure
- Shift work readiness
- Attention to detail

---

# Tier 2 SOC Analyst (Incident Responder)

### Main Responsibilities
- Perform deep investigations on escalated incidents.
- Analyze multiple data sources to determine attack impact.
- Create remediation and recovery plans.
- Develop or improve detection methods.
- Produce detailed incident reports.

### Required Skills
- Strong analytical thinking
- Incident response
- Investigation skills
- Reporting
- Networking & security knowledge
- Communication
- Process discipline
- Working under tight deadlines

# Before Applying

I should:
- Read the full job description.
- Check responsibilities (not just the title).
- Review salary and benefits.
- Check visa/work authorization requirements.
- Apply even if I don't meet every requirement (if I'm a strong fit).

# Interview Preparation

## Research Before Interview
Learn about:
- Company
- Products/services
- SOC team
- Company culture
- Leadership
- Employee reviews
- Retention rate

---

## During the Interview

Remember to:
- Dress professionally.
- Choose a quiet environment.
- Keep my workspace clean.
- Avoid eating or distractions.
- Stay calm and confident.
- Think before answering.

---

# Introducing Myself

My introduction should briefly cover:
- Who I am
- My education
- My cybersecurity journey
- Relevant experience
- Projects
- Certifications
- Career goals

Keep it concise (about 1–2 minutes).

---

# Answering Questions

Instead of giving only "Yes" or "No":
- Explain the situation.
- Describe what I did.
- Mention the outcome.

Use examples from:
- Labs
- Projects
- Internships
- CTFs
- Real-world experiences

# Key Takeaways

- Tier 1 focuses on monitoring and triage.
- Tier 2 focuses on investigation and incident response.
- Strong communication is just as important as technical skills.
- Tailor my resume using keywords from the job description.
- Keep my resume concise and ATS-friendly.
- Research every company before interviewing.
- Prepare examples from my projects to answer behavioral and technical questions.
- Confidence, professionalism, and continuous learning are essential for landing a SOC role.
